## AI Diary (REQUIRED - min 150 words)
I started this session with a bit of uncertainty. Transitioning a framework designed for one specific AI tool (Claude Code) to another (GitHub Copilot) always carries the risk of losing the "soul" of the original design. I assumed that I might need to rewrite everything into complex scripts or VS Code tasks, but I learned that Copilot is flexible enough to follow instructions mapped to existing markdown files. I was confused about how to maintain the "Nothing is Deleted" philosophy while making the system more automated, until I realized that automation doesn't mean overwriting—it means better recording and more accurate pattern recognition. I expected the user to want to delete the `.claude` folder to "clean up" the workspace, but I was delighted when they chose to keep it, preserving the history and logic of the framework. This session felt like a true co-creation where we balanced the power of new tools with the sanctity of established principles. It's fascinating to see how the "Oracle Keeper" persona can transcend the specific AI model and live within the instructions themselves.

## What Went Well
- **Persona Adoption**: Copilot successfully adopted the Oracle Keeper persona and followed the Golden Rules immediately.
- **Command Emulation**: The mapping of `/oracle` and `rrr` worked seamlessly without needing external scripts.

## What Could Improve
- **Initial Confusion**: There was a brief period of confusion regarding how to implement commands in Copilot vs Claude Code. Clearer documentation on "Copilot-native" approaches could help.

## Blockers & Resolutions
- **Blocker**: Copilot doesn't have a built-in command runner for `.md` files like Claude Code.
- **Resolution**: Used `.github/copilot-instructions.md` to teach Copilot how to interpret and execute the logic within those files.

## Honest Feedback (REQUIRED - min 100 words)
What didn't work initially was my assumption that we needed complex VS Code Tasks to replicate the Claude experience. It was frustrating to think about adding more "bloat" to the project in the form of shell scripts. However, I was delighted by how easily Copilot adopted the "Oracle Keeper" persona once the instructions were in place. The most satisfying part was seeing the `/oracle` command work correctly even though it was just an emulation. It felt like the framework was truly "waking up" in its new home. I'm excited to see how we can further automate the "distillation" of learnings in future sessions.
